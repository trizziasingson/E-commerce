Project Overview
Project Aim

This project applies the data science workflow to a large-scale Brazilian e-commerce dataset, aiming to uncover actionable business insights, model customer behavior, and drive process improvement through predictive analytics.

Key Objectives
  • Analyze customer purchase patterns and behavioral trends
  • Segment and profile customer clusters using advanced analytics
  • Model and predict review scores & delivery performance
  • Identify operational bottlenecks and process inefficiencies
  • Generate actionable recommendations for business stakeholders

Analytical Approach & Methodology
Data Integration & Cleaning
  • Imported and merged all tables into a unified analytical dataset
  • Handled missing values and outliers systematically
  • Created derived features for modeling
Exploratory & Predictive Analysis
  • Segmented customers using clustering techniques
  • Analyzed payment preferences and product demand by region
  • Modeled delivery time and review scores with regression and classification algorithms
  • Visualized results using Python and Power BI dashboards
Business Insights
  • Mapped supply chain bottlenecks & delays
  • Identified product and seller performance drivers
  • Delivered actionable recommendations for process optimization
Key Findings & Insights
  • Customer Behavior: High repeat purchase rates in metropolitan regions; payment installment preferences vary by location and demographic.
  • Delivery Performance: Most delays were attributed to logistics partners, with faster delivery correlated to higher review scores.
  • Review Analysis: Detailed text analytics revealed actionable factors driving both positive and negative customer feedback.
  • Product Trends: Top-selling categories reflect regional demand and seasonality; high-value products had longer delivery times on average.
